Behind the Laughter: Exploring the Cosmic Facts of 'The Big Bang Theory' TV Show:

  • Original Title: The show's original title was "Lenny, Penny, and Kenny." It was later changed to "The Big Bang Theory" to emphasize its focus on science and geek culture.
  • Character Origins: Sheldon Cooper's character is loosely based on a computer programmer named Sheldon Kaplan, whom co-creator Chuck Lorre knew in the 1980s. Leonard Hofstadter is named after actor and producer Sheldon Leonard.
  • Theme Song: The show's iconic theme song, "The History of Everything" by Barenaked Ladies, was written specifically for the show. The band members are also big fans of the series.
  • Real-life Scientist Cameos: The show featured guest appearances by real-life scientists, including Stephen Hawking, Neil deGrasse Tyson, and Bill Nye. Stephen Hawking's appearances were particularly notable, showcasing his sense of humor.
  • Mayim Bialik's PhD: Actress Mayim Bialik, who portrayed Amy Farrah Fowler, holds a PhD in neuroscience in real life. Her character's scientific background was not far from her own academic achievements.
  • Soft Kitty: The lullaby "Soft Kitty" that Penny sings to Sheldon when he's sick was inspired by a song co-creator Bill Prady's daughter used to sing. The show later faced a lawsuit over the song's copyright.
  • Set Replicas: There are replicas of the show's apartment sets at Warner Bros. Studios, allowing fans to tour and experience the iconic locations, including Sheldon and Leonard's apartment.
  • Johnny Galecki's Glasses: Leonard's glasses were actually Johnny Galecki's own glasses. He used them as a prop because he felt they were perfect for his character.
  • Penny's Last Name: Penny's last name, Hofstadter, is revealed after she marries Leonard. This mirrors the way the character Mary Cooper, Sheldon's mother, is referred to by her maiden name until she marries.
  • Long-Running Success: "The Big Bang Theory" ran for 12 seasons, making it one of the longest-running sitcoms in television history. It consistently maintained high viewership throughout its run, cementing its place in pop culture.
